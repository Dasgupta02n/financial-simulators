Which layout structure for the SIP Simulator?

This shapes the entire calculator experience. Pick the one that best serves the "anti-marketing" transparency mission.

A

Single-Page Vertical Flow

Inputs at top → Results below → Chart at bottom. One continuous scroll. Simple, but the chart lives far from the sliders — you lose the real-time "slide and see" feedback loop.

B

Split Panel — Left Controls, Right Results

Sliders on the left panel. Live results + chart on the right. Every drag instantly updates the chart. Desktop-first, collapses to vertical on mobile. The "real-time feedback" layout.

C

Dashboard Grid — Cards Everywhere

Each metric (Nominal, Real, Post-Tax, Stress) gets its own card with a mini-chart. Comprehensive but risks the "marketing dashboard" feel we're explicitly avoiding. Many small charts instead of one clear one.

My Recommendation: B

The split panel gives instant visual feedback when dragging sliders — which is the core interaction pattern for a financial simulator. It makes the Real vs Nominal divergence visceral and immediate. On mobile it stacks naturally.
