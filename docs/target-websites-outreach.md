## Financial Simulators (financialsimulators.in)

## Overview

This document identifies 50 Indian websites and platforms where guest posts about SIP returns, EMI analysis, tax optimization, retirement planning, FD comparisons, SWP strategies, FIRE, CTC optimization, NPS, and goal planning would find a receptive audience. The target reader is the Indian middle-class professional who thinks about money the way Amartya Sen thinks about development -- carefully, structurally, and with a bias toward evidence over enthusiasm.

Each entry includes the site name, URL, type, approximate monthly visitors (where available), guest-post status, and the best way to pitch.

It introduces Financial Simulators without making grand claims, proposes a guest post with genuine value, and requests a backlink as a reasonable exchange -- not as an entitlement.

**Subject:** A guest post on [specific topic] for [website name]

Dear [Editor's name or "Editorial Team"],

I write to you from Financial Simulators (financialsimulators.in), a collection of free, data-driven calculators built for Indian middle-class professionals who want to make financial decisions with clarity rather than guesswork.

I have been reading [website name] for some time, and I notice a readership that cares about the same things we do -- evidence over assertion, structure over slogan, and long-term thinking over short-term excitement. This seems like a natural place to contribute something useful.

I would like to propose a guest post on [specific topic, e.g., "How SWP sustainability changes with different withdrawal rates: a calculator-driven analysis"]. The piece would:

## Notes on Pitching

1. **Always pitch before writing.** Send the topic, a brief outline, and why it matters to their readers. Wait for a response.

2. **Read the site first.** Understand their tone, depth, and existing coverage. Do not pitch a topic they have already covered recently, unless you have a genuinely new angle.

3. **Lead with value, not with yourself.** The email should spend 80% of its words on what the article does for their readers, and 20% on who you are.

4. **Follow up once.** If you do not hear back in 7-10 days, send one polite follow-up. Then move on.

5. **Track everything.** Maintain a spreadsheet with: website, contact, date pitched, topic pitched, response, status, and follow-up date.
