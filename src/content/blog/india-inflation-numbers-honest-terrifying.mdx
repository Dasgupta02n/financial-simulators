## The conspiracy that is not one

Every time CPI inflation is announced, someone on social media declares the numbers are manipulated. "Official inflation is 5%, but my grocery bill went up 15%." The impulse is understandable. The conclusion is wrong.

India's CPI is compiled by the National Statistical Office from actual price data collected across 1,114 urban and 1,181 rural markets. The methodology is public, consistent, and aligned with International Labour Organization standards. The numbers are honest.

They are also not what you think they measure.

## The weight problem

CPI assigns weights to different expenditure categories based on average household spending patterns from the 2011-12 consumption survey (updated partially since). Food and beverages carry roughly 45.86% weight. Housing is about 10.07%. Education is 3.47%. Healthcare is 2.69%.

If your household spends 30% on education, 20% on healthcare, and 15% on housing — which describes most urban middle-class families — the CPI basket barely resembles your life. You are experiencing a personal inflation rate that is probably 2-3 percentage points higher than the official number.

This is not conspiracy. It is aggregation. An average of 1.4 billion people's spending patterns will not match any single family's experience. But it does mean that CPI is a macroeconomic indicator, not a personal cost-of-living index.

## Where inflation bites hardest

Education inflation in India has run at 8-10% annually for over a decade. Healthcare costs have risen 10-12% annually. Housing rents in major cities have increased 6-8% annually. These are categories that matter enormously to the middle class and carry low weights in CPI.

Meanwhile, food inflation — which dominates CPI — is volatile but has averaged roughly 5-6%. When food inflation falls, CPI falls, headline news improves. Your school fees and hospital bills did not get the memo.

## What this does to your savings

If your personal inflation rate is 7% and your FD gives 6.4%, you are losing 0.6% in real terms every year. If your personal inflation rate is 8% — not unreasonable for a family with children in private schools — you are losing 1.6% annually.

## The structural truth

India's inflation numbers are among the more transparent in the developing world. The problem is not dishonesty — it is that your life is not average. Calculate your own inflation rate. Track your actual spending categories. Then make financial decisions based on that number, not the headline CPI.

The official number tells you what happened to the average Indian. Your financial plan needs to know what is happening to you.
