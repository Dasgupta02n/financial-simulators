## The comparison that's usually rigged

Every tax season, your CA tells you the old regime saves money. Your HR department defaults you to the new regime. Financial influencers post tables that somehow always confirm whichever regime their affiliate link prefers.

Let's do this properly.

## The new regime (FY 2026-27): what you actually get

After Budget 2026, the new regime slabs look like this:

| Income slab (₹) | Rate |
|---|---|
| 0 – 4,00,000 | 0% |
| 4,00,001 – 8,00,000 | 5% |
| 8,00,001 – 12,00,000 | 10% |
| 12,00,001 – 16,00,000 | 15% |
| 16,00,001 – 20,00,000 | 20% |
| 20,00,001 – 24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above 24,00,000 | 30% |

Standard deduction: ₹75,000. No other deductions. That's the entire deal. What you see is what you pay.

## The old regime: the deduction puzzle

Old regime slabs are steeper — they start at 5% from ₹2.5 lakh and hit 30% at ₹10 lakh. But you can claim deductions: Section 80C (₹1.5L), 80D (₹25K–₹50K), HRA, home loan interest (₹2L), NPS 80CCD(1B) (₹50K). Standard deduction is ₹50,000.

The old regime is a puzzle where your tax bill depends on how many pieces you can assemble. The new regime is a flat menu with no substitutions.

## The honest crossover

At ₹10 lakh salary: new regime wins unless you can find ₹2.5L+ in deductions. At ₹15 lakh salary: the crossover moves to roughly ₹3.5L in deductions. At ₹20 lakh: you need ₹4L+ in deductions for the old regime to win.

Most salaried people claiming 80C max out at ₹1.5L. Add 80D at ₹25K and you're at ₹1.75L — well below the crossover. The old regime only wins if you have a home loan, HRA, or NPS stacking on top.

## The behavioural trap

Here's what nobody mentions: the old regime incentivises spending on specific financial products to save tax. You buy ELSS not because it's the best mutual fund, but because Section 80C demands it. You extend a home loan you could prepay because the interest deduction is too convenient. The new regime strips away that excuse. Your investments must stand on their own merit.

The new regime is the default for a reason: most people save more with it. The old regime is a specialist tool — powerful when you have the deductions to load into it, irrelevant when you don't. Choose based on your actual financial life, not someone else's spreadsheet.
