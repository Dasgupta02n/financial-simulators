## The retirement math most people get wrong

You've saved ₹2 crore. You plan to withdraw ₹1 lakh/month. At 8% returns, the math says your money lasts 25 years.

But what if the market crashes 30% in your first year of retirement?

## The sequence-of-returns problem

When you're withdrawing money, the **order** of returns matters more than the **average** return.

Here's what happens:

- **Good sequence**: Market goes up first, you withdraw less of your corpus, it recovers
- **Bad sequence**: Market crashes early, you're selling more units at lower prices, your corpus shrinks faster

A ₹2 crore corpus with a 30% crash in year 1 and ₹1L/month withdrawals lasts only **17 years** — not 25.

That's **8 years less** of retirement funding.

## What a safe withdrawal rate looks like in India

The 4% rule (from US data) assumes 2-3% inflation. India's inflation averages 5-6%. So:

- **4% rule**: Withdraw ₹8L/year from a ₹2 crore corpus — might not survive high inflation
- **3% rule**: Withdraw ₹6L/year — safer for India, but means a lower monthly income
