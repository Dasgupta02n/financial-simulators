## The headline that travelled far

"Standard deduction doubled to ₹75,000!" This was perhaps the most widely reported tax change from Budget 2024. Headlines made it sound like the government had deposited ₹25,000 into every taxpayer's account.

The reality is more modest.

## What standard deduction actually does

Standard deduction reduces your taxable income by a flat amount — no receipts, no proof, no questions. Under the old regime, it's ₹50,000. Under the new regime (post-Budget 2024), it's ₹75,000.

The tax saved is: deduction amount × your slab rate + cess.

| Slab | Tax saved (old regime, ₹50K) | Tax saved (new regime, ₹75K) |
|---|---|---|
| 5% | ₹2,600 | ₹3,900 |
| 20% | ₹10,400 | ₹15,600 |
| 30% | ₹15,600 | ₹23,400 |

The increase from ₹50K to ₹75K saves you between ₹1,300 (5% slab) and ₹7,800 (30% slab) per year. That's ₹108 to ₹650 per month.

## Who benefits the most

The standard deduction increase exclusively benefits new regime taxpayers — old regime taxpayers still get ₹50,000. If you're in the 30% slab and using the new regime, you save ₹7,800/year compared to the old standard deduction. If you're in the 5% slab, you save ₹1,300. The benefit is progressive by accident — higher earners save more because they always save more from deductions.

## The catch: new regime only

This increased standard deduction applies only to the new regime. If you're in the old regime, your standard deduction remains ₹50,000. The government is using the standard deduction as a lever to make the new regime more attractive — a quiet nudge rather than a dramatic benefit.

## Is it enough to switch regimes?

The ₹25,000 additional deduction is one factor among many. If you're on the margin between old and new regime — say, with ₹3–4L in total deductions — the extra standard deduction might tip the balance. But it's unlikely to be decisive on its own.

Consider someone at ₹15L salary with ₹3.5L in old regime deductions:

- Old regime: taxable income after deductions = ₹11L, tax ≈ ₹1,35,000
- New regime: taxable income after standard deduction = ₹14.25L, tax ≈ ₹1,22,000

The new regime wins by ₹13,000 — and the higher standard deduction is part of that, but not the whole story.

## The bottom line

The doubled standard deduction is real, but the benefit ranges from ₹1,300 to ₹7,800 per year depending on your slab. It is a nudge toward the new regime, not a windfall. Calculate your actual savings before celebrating — or before dismissing it. The number that matters is yours, not the headline's.
