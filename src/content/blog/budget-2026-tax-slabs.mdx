## The question everyone's asking

Budget 2026 raised the basic exemption limit to ₹4 lakh and widened the 5% slab. But what does that actually mean for your monthly salary?

Let's use real numbers.

## Old regime vs new regime: the raw comparison

Under the **old regime** (FY 2025-26), a ₹12 lakh salary with standard deductions gave you roughly ₹9.6 lakh in hand after tax.

Under the **new regime** (Budget 2026 updates), the same ₹12 lakh salary gives you roughly ₹10.2 lakh in hand. That's roughly **₹5,000 more per month**.

But here's the catch: if you claim more than ₹3.75 lakh in deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, home loan interest), the old regime is still better.

## When the old regime wins

If you have:
- ₹1.5L under 80C (PPF, ELSS)
- ₹25K under 80D (health insurance)
- ₹2L home loan interest
- ₹50K NPS under 80CCD(1B)

That's ₹4.25L in deductions. The old regime saves you roughly ₹18,000/year compared to the new one.
