## The asset everyone owns and rarely uses

Indian households hold an estimated 25,000-27,000 tonnes of gold — roughly 11% of the total gold ever mined in human history. We buy it for weddings, for festivals, for security, for status. And then we lock it away and pretend it does not exist until the next wedding.

There is a problem with this arrangement that nobody discusses at the jewellery counter: when you need money urgently, physical gold is among the least liquid and most expensive ways to access it.

## The cost of converting gold to cash

Suppose you have 50 grams of 22-carat gold jewellery bought at ₹6,500 per gram in 2020. Gold prices have risen to roughly ₹7,800 per gram by April 2026. You need ₹3 lakh urgently. Here is what happens:

- **Making charges**: You paid 10-25% above the gold rate as making charges when buying. These are lost entirely on resale.
- **Purity deduction**: The jeweller buying back will test and often deduct 2-5% for purity variance.
- **Buy-sell spread**: Jewellers buy at the wholesale rate and sell at the retail rate. The spread is typically 3-5%.
- **Melting loss**: If selling to a refinery or melting, expect a 1-2% loss.

Net realisation on 50 grams at ₹7,800/gram: you might get roughly ₹3.5-3.6 lakh instead of the ₹3.9 lakh spot value. That is a 8-10% hidden cost on conversion. In an emergency, you cannot negotiate.

## Gold ETFs: the alternative that exists

A Gold ETF tracks the price of 99.5% purity gold and is traded on NSE/BSE. One unit equals 1 gram of gold. You buy and sell at market price during trading hours. The expense ratio is typically 0.35-0.40% annually.

Advantages:

- **Instant liquidity**: Sell on the exchange at 3:00 PM, money in your account by T+1.
- **No making charges, no purity disputes, no buy-sell spread**: You trade at the market price minus a tiny brokerage.
- **No storage cost or risk**: No locker fees, no theft risk, no family disputes about who inherited what.

Disadvantages:

- **No emotional value**: A Gold ETF cannot be worn at a wedding. This is not a joke — in India, the ornamental value of gold is real.
- **Demat account required**: You need a demat and trading account, which costs ₹300-700 per year.
- **Tracking error**: Typically 0.5-1% annually due to the fund's expenses and cash drag.

## Sovereign Gold Bonds: the superior product (if available)

SGBs offer 2.5% annual interest on the investment value, capital appreciation linked to gold prices, and no storage cost. They are issued by the RBI periodically. The catch: the government has indicated it may discontinue SGB issuances as fiscal considerations tighten. If available, SGBs are structurally superior to both physical gold and ETFs for investment purposes.

- **Physical gold**: For ornament and cultural needs. Accept the 8-10% cost on resale as the price of wearing your investment.
- **Gold ETFs**: For the investment component of your gold allocation. Liquid, transparent, low-cost.
- **SGBs**: If available, for the best risk-adjusted gold investment.

A reasonable split: 60% physical (for use), 40% ETF/SGB (for investment liquidity). This respects both the cultural role of gold and the financial need for accessible capital.

## The honest truth

Physical gold is an emotional asset that doubles as a financial one. Gold ETFs are a financial asset with no emotional content. Conflating the two is how families end up with ₹15 lakh in a locker that costs ₹10,000 a year to store and loses 10% when they need to sell. Separate the functions, choose the right vehicle for each, and your gold will serve you better — both at the wedding and in the emergency.
