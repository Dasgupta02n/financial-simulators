## The number everyone quotes

"Start a ₹10,000 SIP today, and in 20 years you'll have ₹1 crore!"

Sounds amazing. But it's not the whole story.

## What actually happens to your money

Let's trace a ₹10,000/month SIP over 20 years at 12% annual returns:

- **Nominal value**: ₹1.02 crore
- **After 6% inflation**: worth about ₹30.5 lakh in today's money
- **After LTCG tax (12.5% above ₹1.25L)**: you keep roughly ₹27.8 lakh in real terms

That's **73% less** than the ₹1 crore headline number.

## Why the gap is so big

Two things eat your returns:

1. **Inflation** — ₹1 crore in 2046 buys what ₹30 lakh buys today. The 6% annual inflation doesn't sound like much, but over 20 years it erodes 70% of your purchasing power.

2. **Tax** — LTCG tax at 12.5% applies to gains above ₹1.25 lakh per year. On a ₹1 crore corpus with ₹90 lakh in gains, that's significant.

## What you can actually do about it

You can't avoid inflation or tax. But you can:

- **Step up your SIP** — a 10% annual increase turns ₹27.8 lakh into ₹55+ lakh in real terms

- **Focus on real returns** — 12% nominal minus 6% inflation = ~5.7% real. That's the number that matters
