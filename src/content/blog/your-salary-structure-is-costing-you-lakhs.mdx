## The decision you didn't make

When you join a company, someone in HR decides your salary structure — the split between basic, HRA, special allowance, and other components. This person has never met you. They don't know your rent, your home loan, or your tax planning. They use a template.

That template is costing you money.

## Why the basic salary percentage matters

Basic salary is the foundation of everything:

- **PF contribution**: 12% of basic, matched by employer
- **HRA calculation**: typically 40–50% of basic
- **Gratuity**: 4.81% of basic
- **Tax deduction at source**: based on taxable components

A high basic means more PF (forced savings), higher HRA exemption potential, and more gratuity. A low basic means more special allowance (fully taxable, no exemptions) and less forced savings.

## Two CTC structures, same number, different outcomes

Consider ₹12L CTC, two structures:

**Structure A (High Basic – 50%)**:
- Basic: ₹5L, HRA: ₹2L, Special: ₹3L, PF: ₹72K, Gratuity: ₹38K
- Monthly in-hand (new regime): ~₹74,500
- Annual PF + gratuity: ₹1,10,500

**Structure B (Low Basic – 30%)**:
- Basic: ₹3L, HRA: ₹1.2L, Special: ₹5.8L, PF: ₹43K, Gratuity: ₹23K
- Monthly in-hand (new regime): ~₹78,000
- Annual PF + gratuity: ₹66,000

Structure B puts ₹3,500 more per month in your bank. Structure A puts ₹44,500 more into retirement savings annually. Neither is objectively better — but the difference is ₹53,000/year in how your money is allocated.

## The old regime variable

Under the old regime, Structure A wins if you rent in a metro city — the higher HRA exemption saves tax. Under the new regime (no HRA exemption), Structure B gives more cash in hand. The optimal structure depends on which regime you choose.

## What you can negotiate

Most companies allow some flexibility in salary structure, especially at mid-to-senior levels. You can request:

- Higher HRA if you pay significant rent
- Lower basic if you want more in-hand (and are in the new regime)
- Meal vouchers, LTA, or fuel allowances — these have specific tax benefits under the old regime

The key is to negotiate the structure, not just the CTC number.
